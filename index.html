Sea Transportation Terms

Bill of Lading is a bill of lading that proves that a transportation contract has been concluded, shows that the goods have been received by the carrier and loaded onto the ship, and that the carrier is obliged to deliver the goods in return for presentation.

demurrage

If the containers supplied by the Carrier are not returned to the Carrier by unloading the cargo by the consignor within the free time specified in the tariff, in accordance with the provisions of the bill of lading, the carrier has the right to demand from the consignor or the shipper and will be charged a delay fee for each day of delay. It is called "container demurrage".

BAF

Fuel expenses of ships will be determined according to the volume and trade balance of the relevant services. BAF tariff will be reviewed every 3 months and will be calculated and applied according to changes in fuel prices. If the change in fuel price is 40 USD/ton more than the previous month, the application will continue monthly.

DBA (Gross Weight Verified) / VGM

Containers on ships used for export purposes are weighed by authorized weighing machine operators, and the weight information (cargo weight, weights of all loading equipment and materials, load protection boards and tare (weight of the container)) is completely entered into the relevant system of the ministry, and this information is not yet loaded onto the ship. It is a concept that expresses the practice of sharing data with the carrier company and port management.
